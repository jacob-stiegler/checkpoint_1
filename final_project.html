Intro

Welcome

This site is a short guide to universal design for the web. It introduces what universal design is, why it matters for real users, and practical techniques you can use when building your own sites.

Who This Site Is For

The content is written for beginning web designers and developers who are learning about accessibility. It assumes basic HTML and CSS knowledge and focuses on how design choices affect real people.

  • Students learning web standards and accessibility basics.
  • Designers who want a quick reminder of core concepts.
  • Developers looking for concrete ways to improve usability.

What You’ll Find on Each Page

  • What – explains the idea of universal design and key concepts like diverse users, information architecture, and assistive technologies.
  • Why – describes why universal design matters for ethics, usability, and reaching more people on the web.
  • How – shows concrete techniques in a table, including semantic HTML, responsive layout, color and contrast, keyboard navigation, and testing.
